PROBLEM

Many hiring managers create an ad regarding some vacancy on job boards or portals online and create online test forms on some other channels in which filtering out the best candidate for the job is a very tiring process. Hiring managers always experience time consuming process in selecting a best candidate through the screening process.

KEY DRIVERS

As there are many onsite onboarding processess for hiring managers to screen a candidate but on some stages, candidate feels shy or they loose intrest during that process. I built my designs to deliver upon the following key points:

  • JOB POSTING
  • A job posting portal where hiring manager can post a job related information in detailed manner and can also define hiring flows for his team. The step-by-step wizard will help hiring manager to create comprehensive job-related details and gather candidate profile data.
  • ONLINE TEST CREATION
  • A solid online test creation experience where hiring manager can design an online test for the candidate for screening purposes. Test includes manual and automatic scoring by system.
  • FILTERING BEST CANDIDATE
  • A detailed ken-ben design of hiring flow experience where the intelligent system filters out best suitable candidate based on automatic scoring by system & manual scoring by reviewers in no time which saves lots of hours of manual screening process.
  • CONDUCT ONLINE VIDEO INTERVIEWS
  • Allowing hiring managers to invite filtered out candidates along with other team members for online video interviews from the same portal.

WHAT DOES THE CURRENT HIRING PROCESS LOOK LIKE?

I was looking to get the clear scenerio of how our primary users went about hiring a candidate through fast onboarding process. In order to get a better understanding of their usage and motivations, here are some research techniques I applied for the redesign.

METHOD 1:
QUALITATIVE & QUANTITATIVE DATA

With the help of performing in-person interviews and conducting surveys, I learnt existing user behaviors for hiring a candidate. Findings included understanding of onboarding process, existing hiring portals and users preferences to shortlist a candidate. Some of the insights are shown below.

METHOD 2:
HR MANAGERS REVIEWS ON DIFFERENT PORTALS

I gone through hundreds of reviews and comments left by hiring managers on different portals, and further categorized and highlighted them in terms of importance. This critical analysis of feedback helped me identify various pain points and areas of improvement.

“Online onboarding process insight report higher levels of influence and are more dependent on the comfortness of candidates and hiring managers”

Hiring managers were always find discomfort from the candidates when they engage them through onsite onboarding process. Due to shyness, these candidates left the interview in between or they felt low in confidence while going through it. In many Scandinavian countries, candidate prefers to go through online onboarding process.

SURVEY FINDINGS

“Creating exam test online and filtering best candidate through ranking system topped the list of hiring process”

JOURNEY MAPPING

Based upon data gathered from in-person interviews, surveys and reviews, I structured my observations and categorized them using a customer journey map. This helped me to identify pain points and areas for improvement throughout the journey. It also helped to initialize discussions to know knowledge gaps and idea generation between team and stakeholders.



CONSIDERING USER NEEDS WITH BUSINESS GOALS

The initial user research uncovered various areas of opportunity, such as creating test and evaluating candidates automatically. However, as the nature of this project was MVP, I kept in mind the buisness goals and resource capacity and decided to work on some key areas, including optimizing user experience around job posting, creating test in quick way, creating hiring flows for the team, reviewing candidates and inviting candidates for video interviews, and thereby, meeting user and business goals including easy hiring and onbaording process, with a strong focus on shortlisting a right candidate to cut down the manual shortlisting process. The previous system also missed the flows and management of the data strategy. The redesign was to reduce these shortcomings.
